ARVP: Camera Mount

ARVP  ·  March 2026 – Present  ·  Mechanical

Gallery
Problem
  • Camera mount required constant reprints to accommodate claw subsystem revisions, with no standardized way to adjust distance
  • No resting position for the claw when not in use, which was burning out the servo responsible for raising it
Proposed Solution
  • Overhaul the existing camera mount with two key additions: a ledge for the claw to rest on, and an adjustable camera distance using a screw-based system
Design Process
  • Iterated on ledge position to find a distance where the claw could sit without blocking its own range of motion
  • Worked closely with the software team on camera height, adjusting positioning until vision models ran cleanly without picking up other parts of the robot as false detections
Final Product
  • Mount is currently deployed on the robot
  • Camera distance adjustable in 7.5mm increments
  • Vision models run successfully without interference from robot hardware
  • Claw ledge is being further iterated on by other members
Next Steps
  • Replace the discrete screw adjustment system with a continuous displacement mechanism while preserving positional accuracy
