High Speed RC Car: Drivetrain

Problem
  • Wanted a project covering the full mechanical engineering process end to end: selection, analysis, manufacturing constraints, and iteration
  • Needed something with real drivetrain dynamics, stress analysis, and DFM considerations baked in from the start
  • Wanted hands-on exposure to simulation tools and heavy mechanical calculations in a context I actually cared about
Proposed Solution
  • Design and build a high-speed RC car entirely from scratch
  • Every component either sourced with engineering justification or designed and manufactured myself, from motor selection through differential
Design Process
  • Started with motor, battery, and ESC selection since those figures set every downstream constraint: torque, RPM, and power budgets
  • Evaluated belt and gear drives for the drivetrain architecture; belt drive was eliminated early due to spatial inefficiency within the chassis envelope
  • Chose a gear system for flexibility: swapping ratios only requires new gears rather than entirely new belts and pulleys
  • Sourced off-the-shelf gears where possible to keep the design manufacturable and maintainable, then built around those constraints
  • Worked through torque at each stage, Lewis bending stress on gear teeth, shaft torsion, and bearing load cases for validation
  • Several components failed preliminary analysis; the motor shaft failure sent me back to electronics selection entirely, which taught me the importance of the feedback loop between analysis and design
  • Designed the differential from scratch: started with the kinematics of how an open differential distributes torque, then translated that into something manufacturable
  • Made the differential fully modular so internals can be accessed and serviced independently
  • Currently at roughly 15 CAD iterations and 3 printed prototypes; running FEA in Ansys to validate stress concentrations in the differential housing and planet gear interfaces
Final Product
  • High-speed RC car currently in late-stage design and validation
  • Electronics stack integrated and operational
  • Drivetrain architecture finalized with a custom-designed modular differential
  • Awaiting FEA sign-off before committing to metal fabrication
Next Steps
  • Complete FEA validation on the differential housing and planet gear interfaces
  • Build a full vehicle dynamics model with suspension to optimize handling and load distribution
  • Machine final metal components and assemble
