ARVP: Claw Retractor

ARVP  ·  April 2026 – Present  ·  Mechanical

Gallery
Problem
  • Claw ledge worked on dryland but consistently failed to catch the claw during pool testing
  • Servo keeps burning out and replacements are not a sustainable fix
  • No FETs for the claw on the main board, so the claw cannot be powered off when not in use
  • Needed a passive mechanical solution to hold the claw up while still allowing it to lower on demand
  • One week turnaround before the next pool test
Proposed Solution
  • Initial idea was a rubber band strapped between the claw and the robot frame, keeping the arm up passively
Design Process
  • Rubber band idea got cut quickly since rubber degrades underwater, but the concept of a passive restoring force was sound
  • Pivoted to a spring; mechanical sub-team lead suggested a strong magnet instead so the claw would automatically clamp to the hull in the upright position
  • Ran calculations using the local kill-switch magnets at roughly 30lbf, preliminary test worked well
  • Had to abandon the magnet approach after it was found to interfere with the Doppler Velocity Logger
  • Went back to the spring idea using tensioned springs from the garage
  • Designed two mounting iterations to find the best spring attachment position, which needed to be collinear or slightly offset from the claw mounting point on the servo
  • Tested multiple springs with unknown specs, settled on using two in combination based on feel and rough napkin math on spring constant
  • Estimated spring placement on the arm from that calculation and tested
Final Product
  • System is currently on the robot
  • Arm holds up correctly and lowers without issue
  • Servo did not burn out after being held in the upright position for over 24 hours, which had never been achieved before
Next Steps
  • Properly characterize the springs and run optimized placement calculations
  • Reduce to one spring once specs are confirmed
  • Test the sheet metal bracket for a lower mounting position
  • Reduce the overall size of the system
