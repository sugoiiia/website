High Speed RC Car: Wheel Assemblies

Personal Project  ·  2025  ·  Mechanical

Gallery
Problem
  • Off-the-shelf wheel assemblies didn't suit a double wishbone suspension, which needs custom uprights with specific mounting points for A-arms, steering links, and push/pull rods
  • Material was constrained to aluminum stock from the Elko Engineering Garage, with a maximum face of 2in x 2in, so the design had to fit within that envelope
Proposed Solution
  • Design a custom upright from scratch that works across all four wheels with a single differential design and all necessary mounting points
Design Process
  • Started with the 50mm x 50mm spatial constraint and sketched out what the geometry could realistically look like
  • Recognized early that designing a unique differential per wheel wasn't feasible, so the goal became one differential design that mounts cleanly to every corner
  • Settled on a cloverleaf shape for the assembly
  • Built a rough vehicle dynamics model in SMath to estimate peak upright loading, arriving at 50N as a conservative upper bound
  • First FEA run in Ansys flagged the A-arm mounting point thickness as critically undersized, with max stress well above the yield stress of 6061 aluminum
  • Redesigned: reduced main hub thickness, increased mounting point thickness, removed redundant material
  • Weight reduced by roughly 15% in the process
  • Redesign passed FEA validation
Final Product
  • Fully validated wheel upright with complete assembly: couplings, bearings, and wheels sourced and confirmed compatible
Next Steps
  • Design suspension geometry and validate that the A-arm travel works with the current upright design
  • CNC machine the uprights and assemble
